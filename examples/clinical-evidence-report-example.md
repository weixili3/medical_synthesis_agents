## Introduction

Type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by hyperglycemia, insulin resistance, and impaired insulin secretion. Effective management of T2DM requires a multifaceted approach, including lifestyle modifications, medication, and regular monitoring. Telemedicine interventions, encompassing remote monitoring, video consultations, and mobile health applications, have emerged as a promising strategy to improve diabetes care, particularly in underserved populations. This clinical evidence synthesis aims to evaluate the effectiveness of telemedicine interventions in managing T2DM, focusing on glycemic control, quality of life, and other relevant clinical outcomes.

## Discussion

The findings of this clinical evidence synthesis suggest that telemedicine interventions can be an effective strategy for managing Type 2 diabetes. Telemedicine offers several advantages over traditional in-person care, including increased accessibility, convenience, and cost-effectiveness. These interventions may be particularly beneficial for individuals in underserved communities or rural areas with limited access to healthcare services. The results are consistent with existing guidelines that recommend the use of telemedicine for diabetes management. However, there are several limitations to consider.
